About Me | My Back Story

My backstory is kind of hefty and has a ton of things that I just do not feel comfortable telling right now. However, I will give you a good idea of what my backstory is. Going far back, during middle school, I was considered to be an outcast, I was beaten up a ton and I am pretty sure every single person in that school hated me. I mean, no different than your typical teenage life ( according to this anyway ). From there, I ended up making friends with someone who guaranteed success through game hacking. I will not specify which games, but he brought me onto this team of people that would give us cheats and would pay us to test them and report bugs or issues within the programs as a user. Given the money I was making and the fact that I had just a single friend I could count on, I kept working for them and eventually got to the point of development positions for specific systems even if I did not know what they were doing. One day this all crashed and ended up putting me in a rough spot. The reason? The team and said friend had been working for, we can call them CompanyGhost was apparently stealing source code from major exploit providers and essentially “selling” them off. The way those teams had found out is by someone being able to infiltrate the organization’s chats and servers and learning about what they did. The organization that decided to hit us decided to doxx and release all public information, then swatted every single person they could. Luckily, I was not hit but from then on I never touched a computer until a few months later. After I had gotten back onto the whole security train, I made an oath to myself that I would never steal code from people, especially after people online I had at the time warned me that it was a bad habit. I also told myself that I would try to study as hard as I could to make a genuine living in this field and if I wanted to do something for myself and show my worth, it would be this. I made security my entire powerhouse for anger, rage, or any pain or issues I had suffered through in my life and would put it all into studying, hacking or just breaking firmware for no reason. Through all this learning I then decided to spin up communities and start spreading my stories to better motivate kids who were in a similar position in life or who had been put down by hundreds around them. I still to this day run multiple communities where I try to educate younger generations about the ethics of hacking and programming and try to get as many kids and even adults up off the ground! I have spent my time in this community also advocating for drug or substance addict recovery centers, have contributed to podcasts about mental health, and have even spoken and acted in multiple organizations to help people out of the hole of substance abuse, self-harm, and other various extreme forms of mental stress. I do this simply because I believe that even if they are drug addicts, or even if they are on the street, or even if they are considered “poor” by standards, they have a very very big chance to live life again and get back up!

That is the small gist of my story- I could write a book on it if I wanted to but that is all I will go into for today.
